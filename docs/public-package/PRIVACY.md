# Privacy

Open Flint Atlas maps places, systems, services, and public conditions. Do not map people as targets.

## Public Read Model Rule

Public exports may include only reviewed, source-grounded, place-oriented data.
They must not include contributor identity, private reviewer notes, raw
submission text, original photos, EXIF metadata, exact identifying timestamps,
or household-level implications from community reports.

## Community Reports

Community reports are private at intake. A report can become public only as a
reviewer-written safe summary attached to a public place id, a status, a
confidence label, source ids, a review date, and a caveat.

## Private Fields

The following categories are private by default:

- Contributor name, email, phone, and IP address.
- Raw report text.
- Photo originals, faces, license plates, interiors, and EXIF metadata.
- Exact submit timestamps when they can identify a person.
- Private reviewer notes and internal moderation reasons.
- Address-level service line records or household-level implications.

## Redaction

Before publication, reviewers must strip direct identifiers, rewrite raw
reports as safe summaries, downsample exact timestamps to dates, remove EXIF
metadata, and blur faces and license plates if images are ever supported.

## Official Conflicts

When a resident observation conflicts with an official or public source, the
atlas should mark the conflict and show caveats. It must not automatically turn
the observation into truth.
